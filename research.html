Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of education and technology, with a dash of human-computer interaction. I am mainly interested in understanding what happens in the (physical) classroom, and in blended learning settings that combine face-to-face and online activities. With that understanding, my aim is to support teachers and learners with novel technologies that support them in their everyday learning. I’ve paid special attention to more complex, active methods of learning such as collaborative learning, or the provision of timely feedback through learning analytics. In order to do that, I have used a variety of technologies and methods, from very simple questionnaires aimed at reflection, to community-oriented platforms to support learning design by teachers, augmented paper tabletops, or cutting-edge mobile eye-tracking technology.
